[CONCEPT]

FROM ICON
TO CONDITION

The Blue Marble once framed Earth as a unified and stable whole. This project reworks that image as a contemporary condition, where plastic is no longer external waste but embedded across environmental, atmospheric and biological systems.

[1972 / IMAGE]

THE BLUE
MARBLE

Captured during the Apollo 17 mission in 1972, The Blue Marble became a symbol of Earth as a single, fragile whole. From space, the planet appeared complete, borderless and natural, shaping how it was imagined through unity and environmental care.
This project returns to that image differently. Rather than reading Earth as whole, it looks beneath the surface, where plastic moves through systems of extraction, circulation, waste and contamination.

The image begins as unity.
The system beneath reveals instability.
EMBEDDING

Plastic begins as something external, but becomes embedded within planetary systems.

DETECTION

These particles are detected across oceans, organisms and biological systems.

SYSTEM SHIFT

The planet shifts from image to system, where plastic becomes part of the Earth’s material layer.

[RESEARCH]

PLASTIC IS
NO LONGER
EXTERNAL

Microplastics circulate through oceans, soil and the atmosphere. They are detected in living organisms and have entered the human body. Plastic is no longer external to the planet, but internal, ongoing and difficult to reverse.

[CIRCULATION / TRACE]
WHAT CANNOT BE
SEEN IS NOT
ABSENT
[SPECIMEN ANALYSIS]
At a smaller scale, plastic becomes harder to see but harder to separate. Particles, fibres and fragments are detected as traces within biological and environmental systems.
[IMPACT / INTERNAL]
PLASTIC IS NOW
INTERNAL
The project shifts scale from the planet to the body. Microplastics enter through food, water and air, and have been detected in blood, lungs and tissue. Plastic becomes biological, not just environmental.

[SYSTEM REDEFINITION]
THE WORLD IS
NO LONGER A
SURFACE
The Blue Marble no longer holds true as a clean image of a contained planet. Plastic does not stay where it is placed. It disperses, enters, accumulates and remains. The planet is not only something we look at. It is a system we are already inside.
